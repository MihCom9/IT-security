How Hackers Hack Us

Hackers employ various methods to gain unauthorized access to systems and data. Here are three different ways they can attack us:

  1. Cracking Wi-Fi Passwords

    Hackers can crack Wi-Fi passwords using several techniques:

    Dictionary Attack: Hackers use a precompiled list of commonly used passwords and systematically test each one against the Wi-Fi network until they find a match.

    Brute Force Attack: They try every possible combination of characters until they successfully guess the password. This method can be time-consuming but effective against weak passwords.

    WPS Attack: Wi-Fi Protected Setup (WPS) is a feature intended to simplify the process of connecting devices to a secure wireless network. However, it has vulnerabilities that hackers can exploit to gain access to the network.

  2. Brute Forcing Email Passwords

    Brute force attacks involve systematically guessing passwords until the correct one is found:

    Automated Tools: Hackers use automated tools that can rapidly test millions of password combinations.

    Credential Stuffing: Using stolen username and password pairs from previous data breaches, hackers attempt to gain access to other accounts that might use the same credentials.

  3. Breaking into Phones

    Hackers can compromise phones through various methods without the user's knowledge:

    Phishing: Hackers send deceptive messages (via SMS, email, or social media) that trick users into clicking malicious links or downloading malware.

    Learn more about Phishing >

    Phishing is a method where hackers trick users into revealing sensitive information:

    Email Phishing: Hackers send emails that appear to be from trusted sources, prompting users to click on malicious links or download infected attachments.

    Whaling: This targets high-profile individuals like executives or celebrities, with emails tailored to their interests or responsibilities.

    Smishing and Vishing: Similar to email phishing, but using SMS (smishing) or voice calls (vishing) to deceive victims into providing personal information or performing certain actions.

    Malware: Installing malicious apps can give hackers control over a phone. They can install spyware to monitor activities, keyloggers to capture keystrokes, or ransomware to lock the phone and demand payment.

    SIM Swapping: : HHackers trick or bribe mobile carriers into transferring the victim's phone number to a SIM card they control. This can allow them to intercept SMS-based two-factor authentication codes and gain access to the victim’s accounts.

Stay informed, use strong passwords, and be cautious online to protect yourself from cyber threats.
